Vosoritide in Achondroplasia

6 May 2026

Part 1 — Biology & Pathophysiology

FGFR3: The Culprit Gene

🧬 The Mutation

Over 97% of cases: p.Gly380Arg gain-of-function mutation in FGFR3 transmembrane domain (chr4p16.3). >80% arise de novo. Autosomal dominant.

⚙️ Downstream Signalling

Constitutive FGFR3 activation → STAT-1 pathway (↓ chondrocyte proliferation & differentiation) + MAPK/RAS-ERK pathway (↓ extracellular matrix). Both suppress endochondral ossification.

🦴 Net Effect

Disproportionate short-limbed stature (rhizomelia). Long bones severely shortened; intramembranous ossification (skull) relatively spared → characteristic macrocephaly and frontal bossing.

Medical Complications

🧠Foramen magnum stenosis / sudden death risk
😴Obstructive sleep apnea
🦵Spinal stenosis / lumbar hyperlordosis
🦷Recurrent otitis media
💧Hydrocephalus (communicating)
🚶Genu varum / gait abnormalities
😔Reduced QoL, psychosocial burden

Incidence: 1 in 20,000–30,000 live births. Global prevalence 1–9/100,000

Part 1 — Pre-Vosoritide Era

Traditional Management: Unmet Need

💉
Growth Hormone (rhGH)
  • ✅ Modest ↑ growth velocity yr 1
  • ⚠️ Effect diminishes yr 2 onwards
  • ❌ No documented final height gain
  • ❌ Worsens body disproportion
  • ❌ Not disease-modifying

Adult height gain: only +2.8 cm (F), +3.5 cm (M)

🔩
Limb Lengthening Surgery
  • ✅ Can add 30–35 cm height
  • ✅ Improves proportionality
  • ❌ Invasive; multiple surgeries
  • ❌ High complication rates
  • ❌ Pin tract infections, nerve injury
  • ❌ Not disease-modifying

High complication/revision rates; only at specialist centres

🩺
Complication Management
  • ✅ Foramen magnum decompression
  • ✅ CPAP for OSA
  • ✅ Lumbar laminectomy
  • ✅ VP shunting (hydrocephalus)
  • ✅ ENT intervention (otitis)

Treats complications only — not the underlying pathway

🚫
The Common Failure

All three approaches — GH, surgery, and complication management — leave the constitutively active FGFR3 receptor completely untouched. None interrupts the STAT-1/MAPK signalling cascade driving growth suppression.

🎯
The Unmet Need — Until 2021

A treatment that targets the molecular root cause, improves growth proportionately, and is safe for long-term use from infancy. This is exactly the gap vosoritide — a CNP analog targeting the NPR-B/FGFR3 axis — was designed to fill.

Part 1 — Normal Physiology

CNP: The Body's Natural Growth Regulator — and the FGFR3 Balance

🌿 What CNP Does Normally
📍
Locally produced — paracrine, not hormonal
Made by growth plate chondrocytes themselves. Acts on neighbouring cells. Does NOT circulate systemically like ANP or BNP.
🔗
Binds NPR-B — bone-selective receptor
NPR-B (guanylyl cyclase-B) is highly expressed on chondrocytes. CNP has far higher affinity for NPR-B than ANP or BNP — making this a bone-specific signal.
Drives the full chondrocyte maturation sequence:
↑ Proliferation
Builds chondrocyte columns in proliferative zone
↑ Hypertrophy
10× cell enlargement pushes bone ends apart
↑ ECM Synthesis
Collagen X, proteoglycans — mineralisation scaffold
⊣ FGFR3/MAPK
Naturally restrains FGFR3 braking signal via RAF1
Genetic proof: CNP-knockout mice → severe dwarfism (growth plate phenotype). CNP-overexpressing mice → skeletal overgrowth. (Chusho et al., PNAS 2001; Yasoda et al., Nat Med 2004)

Part 2 — Development History

The Road to Vosoritide

2012–2014

BioMarin engineers BMN-111 (vosoritide): recombinant 39-AA CNP analog resistant to neutral endopeptidase → stable enough for daily dosing

2015

Phase 2 proof-of-concept: positive results in children with ACH. Orphan Drug Designation (USA & EU). Dose-finding completed.

2017–2020

Phase 3 (Study 111-301): 121 children, ages 5–18, randomised double-blind placebo-controlled. Published The Lancet, September 2020.

Aug 2021 / Nov 2021

EMA approved (initial: ≥2 yrs, expanded to ≥4 months 2023) · FDA approved (≥5 yrs, expanded to all ages Oct 2023) — FIRST targeted therapy for achondroplasia. Breakthrough Therapy & Rare Pediatric Disease designations.

Oct 2023

FDA expanded label: ALL AGES from birth (open epiphyses). >10,000 patient-years of cumulative data by 2026.

Part 3 — Clinical Evidence

Landmark Phase 3 Trial (Lancet, 2020)

Study Design (111-301)

Randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicentre Phase 3. 121 participants, ages 5–18 years, open epiphyses. Vosoritide 15 µg/kg/day SC vs placebo. Duration: 52 weeks.

+1.57
cm/year AGV increase
vs placebo (p<0.0001)
+0.27
Height Z-score change
at 52 weeks
Key Finding

The ~2 cm/yr AGV deficit vs average-stature children was substantially reduced. Consistent results across all subgroup analyses. First evidence of a precision therapy targeting the root cause of achondroplasia.

6-Year Follow-up (2025)
Boys AGV gain/yr +1.84 cm
Girls AGV gain/yr +1.44 cm

Deaths / LT harm None
2026 Meta-analysis

10 real-world studies, 696 children. Height Z-score: +0.32 at 12 months. Confirms trial findings in real-world practice.

Part 3 — Safety Profile

Adverse Effects & Clinical Monitoring

⚠️ Most Common AEs (>10%)

Injection site erythema Most common
Injection site reactions (swelling, bruising, pain) Mild, local
Transient hypotension ~13% (trial data)
Vomiting Transient

⚠️ Infant-Specific Alert

Cardiovascular AEs (symptomatic transient hypotension) reported in infants — particularly if not adequately fed before injection. Extended fasting interval worsens risk. Ensure feeding within 30–60 min before injection per regional label.

✅ Monitoring Guidance

🩺Blood pressure monitoring at first injections and dose changes
🍼Adequate hydration and feeding before each injection
📈Height velocity: every 3–6 months
🦴Bone age and growth plate status annually
💊Reconstitute fresh before each injection; no preservatives

✅ Long-term Safety

No long-term safety signals at 6 years follow-up (464 person-years exposure). No deaths. Over 10,000 patient-years of real-world data as of March 2026 confirm favourable safety profile.

Part 4 — Clinical Practice

Practical Considerations for Clinicians

🧒 When to Start?

Early treatment increasingly supported by evidence. FDA-approved for all ages with open growth plates (Oct 2023); EMA: ≥4 months; Japan/Australia: birth. Earlier start = longer treatment window before epiphyseal closure. BioMarin data (2026 ACMG): early treatment yields durable improvements in proportionality, arm span, and BMI.

📏 Setting Expectations

Vosoritide does NOT cure achondroplasia or normalise stature. Goal: improve growth trajectory, reduce complications burden, improve proportionality. Final adult height data still awaited. Counsel families on realistic expectations.

🏥 Multidisciplinary Care

Continue ACH complication surveillance: foramen magnum MRI/screening in infancy, polysomnography for OSA, ENT review, neuroimaging for hydrocephalus. Vosoritide treats growth — complications need separate management. Ongoing Study 111-209 (Phase 2, infants 0–12 months at CMC risk) is specifically investigating whether very early vosoritide treatment can reduce the need for cervicomedullary decompression surgery by expanding the foramen magnum synchondroses during the critical window of skull base development.

Response Assessment

📐Height velocity q3–6 months; compare with ACH-specific growth charts
📊Upper-to-lower segment ratio — significant change often takes 2+ years vs earlier AGV improvement
🦴Annual bone age; epiphyseal status
💨6-minute walk test: retrospective data suggest improved functional capacity

Part 5 — Pipeline

Novel & Emerging Treatments

💊
Infigratinib (BridgeBio)
FGFR3 tyrosine kinase inhibitor — ORAL
  • 🔬 Directly inhibits FGFR3 kinase activity
  • 📊 PROPEL 2 trial (Cohort 5): +2.51 cm/yr AHV at 12 months (p=0.0015)
  • 📐 Significant proportionality improvement at 18 months
  • 🏆 FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation — Sept 2024
  • 🌟 First-ever oral therapy for ACH in development
  • ✅ Once-daily oral tablet — potentially better adherence
🗓️
Navepegritide (YUVIWEL®) — FDA Approved
Long-acting CNP prodrug — ONCE WEEKLY
  • 🔬 CNP prodrug: designed for sustained CNP exposure
  • 🎉 FDA Accelerated Approval Feb 27, 2026 — children ≥2 yrs with open epiphyses
  • 🏥 Pivotal ApproaCH Trial: published JAMA Pediatrics Nov 2025 — met primary endpoint
  • 📐 Improved lower-limb alignment, proportionality & HRQoL
  • 📅 Once-weekly injection — major adherence advantage vs daily
  • ✅ Low injection site reaction rate; no symptomatic hypotension
🧪
Meclizine / Meclozine
Repurposed antihistamine — ORAL — early phase
  • 🔬 Downstream RAS–ERK pathway inhibitor
  • 💊 Original indication: motion sickness / vertigo
  • 🔭 Drug repurposing strategy; preclinical evidence
  • ⚠️ Early-phase research; no pivotal data yet
🔭
Horizon: Gene & Antisense Therapy
Future directions — preclinical
  • 🧬 FGFR3-targeting antisense oligonucleotides

Summary

Key Take-Home Messages

🎯

Vosoritide is the FIRST targeted, disease-modifying therapy for achondroplasia — directly addresses the FGFR3 gain-of-function pathophysiology via the NPR-B/cGMP axis.

📊

Robust evidence base: Phase 3 (Lancet 2020) +1.57 cm/yr; 6-year LT data (+5.75 cm at 3 yrs); 2026 meta-analysis (696 children) confirms real-world efficacy and favourable safety profile.

📐

Beyond height: Vosoritide improves body proportionality, upper-to-lower segment ratio, and potentially functional outcomes — outcomes more meaningful than height alone.

👶

Start early: FDA-approved for all ages with open growth plates (Oct 2023); EMA ≥4 months; Japan/Australia: birth. Earlier treatment maximises window before epiphyseal fusion. Manage infant-specific hypotension risk with feeding timing.

🚀

Evolving landscape: Oral infigratinib (BTD; +2.51 cm/yr) and once-weekly navepegritide (YUVIWEL®, FDA Accelerated Approval Feb 2026, ≥2 yrs) now offers an approved alternative — two CNP-based therapies are now approved for achondroplasia. Infigratinib (oral FGFR3 inhibitor) has positive Phase 3 PROPEL 3 data (Feb 2026) with NDA/MAA submissions planned H2 2026 — still investigational.
